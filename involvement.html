    • Orchestral Involvement

    The music timeline displays key information about the piece in one-second intervals. The height of the music timeline at a given moment encodes the orchestral involvement, or the number of instruments playing, at that time. The intensity of the color encodes the velocity, or the intensity of sound based on the attack of the notes, at that time. This allows one to instantly compare the most intense moments of the piece with the softer and more intimate moments.

    The arrow in the top-right corner allows one to view from what direction the piece's audio comes from within a given interval, on average. This helps get a quick view into where one may have to conduct at a given time.
